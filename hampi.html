Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in the state of Karnataka, India. It was once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century, and its ruins are a testimony to its rich cultural and architectural heritage. The site is known for its stunning temples, intricately carved stone structures, and expansive royal enclosures. Key attractions include the iconic Virupaksha Temple, the Vijaya Vittala Temple, and the Stone Chariot. Hampi also offers beautiful landscapes with boulder-strewn hills and the peaceful Tungabhadra River flowing through the region.
